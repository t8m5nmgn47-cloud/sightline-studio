What we check

Three things every local business should know.

We look at your business the way the public already can — across three areas — and turn it into one report you can actually act on.

Your website

Does it help or hurt?

Is it fast, does it work on phones, and are there broken pages or trust-killers that quietly send customers away?

“Your contact page takes 9 seconds to load on mobile — most visitors leave by three.”

Your exposure

Is the front door locked?

Using only public information, we flag things that are visible to anyone but probably shouldn’t be.

“An old admin login page is publicly reachable and easy to find.”

Your reputation

What people are saying.

Your reviews, ratings, and mentions across the web — pulled into one place, with what’s new and what’s slipping.

“Two recent two-star reviews mention the same wait-time issue.”

Your website, in detail

  • Load speed on phones and desktops
  • Mobile-friendliness and obvious layout breaks
  • Secure connection (HTTPS) and certificate health
  • Broken links and dead pages
  • Basic search-visibility and trust signals

Your exposure, in detail

  • Email spoofing protection (is your address protected from being faked?)
  • Publicly reachable login or admin pages
  • Out-of-date software visible from the outside
  • Company emails caught in known data breaches
  • Look-alike domains set up to impersonate you

Your reputation, in detail

  • Star ratings across the major review sites
  • Recurring themes in what customers say
  • Reviews going unanswered
  • Listings that are wrong or out of date
  • Public mentions worth knowing about

What we don’t do

  • We never log in or access private systems
  • We never run intrusive scans or “attacks”
  • We don’t profile or name individual people
  • We don’t promise to make you “100% secure”
  • It’s a clear snapshot — not a guarantee
