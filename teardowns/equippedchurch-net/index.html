What the Sightline church audit measures

Generic website audits miss what actually matters for faith communities. Sightline’s church-specific teardown evaluates seven areas that directly affect whether a first-time visitor decides to walk in the door — or keeps driving.

  • Visitor clarity — Can a first-time visitor answer “what will Sunday look like for me?” within 10 seconds of landing?
  • Mobile experience — 70–80% of church web traffic is mobile. Is the site fast, thumb-friendly, and readable without zooming?
  • Kids & family transparency — Parents need to know their children are safe. Is check-in, volunteer vetting, and kids programming clearly explained?
  • Online giving UX — Is giving easy to find, frictionless to complete, and available as a recurring option on any device?
  • Sermon & media discoverability — Can a curious visitor watch or listen to a recent message before committing to attend in person?
  • Connect card & follow-up — Is there a simple, low-friction way for first-time visitors to indicate interest so the church can reach out warmly?
  • Technical foundations — HTTPS, mobile load speed, Church structured-data schema, and local search discoverability for “church near me” searches.

Why church websites underperform

Most church websites were built by volunteers with limited web experience, or on templated platforms optimized for other industries. Common gaps include: no clear first-visit guide, buried service times, kids-ministry information that’s hard to find, giving links that require multiple taps on mobile, and sermon archives that are impossible to navigate.

A Sightline rebuild addresses all of these with a single, purpose-built template designed specifically for faith communities — visitor-first, mobile-first, and built to convert curiosity into connection.
